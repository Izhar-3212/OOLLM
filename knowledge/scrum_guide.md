# Scrum Framework

## Definition

Scrum is a lightweight framework for developing, delivering, and sustaining complex products through empiricism and iterative delivery of value. Scrum is the most widely used Agile framework. Scrum is intentionally incomplete: it defines only the parts required to implement Scrum theory, and teams build their own processes on top of it.

Scrum enables teams to:

* Deliver value incrementally.
* Adapt to changing requirements.
* Reduce risk through frequent inspection and adaptation.
* Improve continuously.

---

## Scrum Theory

Scrum is founded on Empiricism.

Empiricism asserts that knowledge comes from experience and decisions are made based on observation.

Scrum relies on three pillars:

### Transparency

Work, progress, goals, and quality standards must be visible and understood.

### Inspection

Artifacts, progress, and outcomes must be inspected frequently.

### Adaptation

When deviations or opportunities are identified, plans and processes should be adjusted quickly.

---

## Scrum Values

The five Scrum values are Commitment, Courage, Focus, Openness, and Respect. Successful use of Scrum depends on people living these five values. They give direction to the Scrum Team's work, actions, and behavior, and they build the trust that transparency, inspection, and adaptation require.

### Commitment

Commitment is the Scrum value of dedicating yourself to achieving the goals of the Scrum Team and supporting each other.

### Courage

Courage is the Scrum value of doing the right thing, addressing difficult problems, and making necessary decisions.

### Focus

Focus is the Scrum value of concentrating on the Sprint Goal and the highest-value work of the Sprint.

### Openness

Openness is the Scrum value of sharing progress, challenges, and information transparently with the team and stakeholders.

### Respect

Respect is the Scrum value of treating each other as capable, independent people with valuable expertise and differing perspectives.

## Scrum Team

### Characteristics

* Self-managing
* Cross-functional
* Small enough to remain agile
* Accountable for creating value each Sprint

Recommended size:

* Typically 10 or fewer people

---

## Accountabilities

### Product Owner

Purpose:

Maximize product value.

Responsibilities:

* Define and communicate Product Goal.
* Manage Product Backlog.
* Order Product Backlog items.
* Ensure backlog transparency.
* Make value-based prioritization decisions.

Authority:

* Final authority over Product Backlog ordering.

---

### Scrum Master

Purpose:

Establish Scrum effectiveness.

Responsibilities:

* Coach Scrum adoption.
* Facilitate Scrum events.
* Remove impediments.
* Improve organizational understanding of Scrum.
* Support self-management.
* Foster continuous improvement.

Authority:

* Process leadership.
* Coaching and facilitation.

---

### Developers

Purpose:

Create valuable increments.

Responsibilities:

* Deliver Done increments.
* Create Sprint plans.
* Maintain quality.
* Adapt plans daily.
* Manage technical execution.

Authority:

* Decide how work is performed.
* Self-organize around Sprint execution.

---

## Scrum Artifacts

### Product Backlog

Definition:

Ordered list of future product work.

Characteristics:

* Emergent
* Continuously refined
* Value-driven
* Single source of product work

Commitment:

Product Goal

---

### Sprint Backlog

Definition:

Selected Product Backlog Items plus delivery plan.

Contains:

* Sprint Goal
* Selected work
* Execution plan

Ownership:

Developers

Commitment:

Sprint Goal

---

### Increment

Definition:

Integrated, usable product outcome created during a Sprint.

Requirements:

* Meets Definition of Done.
* Provides value.
* Is usable.
* Can be released immediately if desired.

Commitment:

Definition of Done

---

## Commitments

### Product Goal

Purpose:

Long-term target for the product.

Characteristics:

* Future state of the product.
* Guides backlog decisions.
* One active Product Goal at a time.

Associated Artifact:

Product Backlog

---

### Sprint Goal

Purpose:

Single objective for the Sprint.

Characteristics:

* Provides flexibility in execution.
* Creates focus.
* Guides decision-making.

Associated Artifact:

Sprint Backlog

---

### Definition of Done

The Definition of Done is a formal description of the state of the Increment when it meets the quality measures and quality standard required for the product. Work is only considered complete when it satisfies the Definition of Done; the moment a Product Backlog item meets it, an Increment is born.

The Definition of Done creates transparency by giving everyone a shared, transparent understanding of what "complete" means. If a Product Backlog item does not meet the Definition of Done, it cannot be released or presented at the Sprint Review.

Characteristics:

* Explicit
* Transparent
* Measurable
* Consistently applied by the entire Scrum Team

Associated Artifact:

Increment

---

## Scrum Events

All Scrum events are time-boxed.

Purpose:

* Transparency
* Inspection
* Adaptation

---

### Sprint

Definition:

Container event for all Scrum activities.

Maximum Duration:

1 month

Outputs:

* Valuable Increment
* Progress toward Product Goal

Rules:

* Duration remains consistent.
* Quality does not decrease.
* Scope may be clarified and renegotiated.
* Sprint Goal remains stable.

Cancellation:

Only the Product Owner may cancel a Sprint.

---

### Sprint Planning

Purpose:

Plan Sprint work.

Maximum Duration:

8 hours for a one-month Sprint.

Outputs:

* Sprint Goal
* Sprint Backlog
* Initial delivery plan

Questions:

1. Why is this Sprint valuable?
2. What can be accomplished?
3. How will the work be completed?

Participants:

Entire Scrum Team

---

### Daily Scrum

The Daily Scrum is a 15-minute time-boxed event for the Developers to inspect progress toward the Sprint Goal and adapt the Sprint Backlog, producing an actionable plan for the next 24 hours of work. It is held at the same time and place every working day of the Sprint.

Maximum Duration:

15 minutes

Participants:

Developers

Outputs:

* Updated plan for next 24 hours
* Adapted Sprint Backlog

Focus:

Progress toward Sprint Goal. The Daily Scrum is a planning meeting for the Developers, not a status report to management.

---

### Sprint Review

Purpose:

Inspect Increment and gather feedback.

Maximum Duration:

4 hours for a one-month Sprint.

Participants:

Scrum Team and Stakeholders

Outputs:

* Product feedback
* Product Backlog adaptations
* Future opportunities

Focus:

Value delivered

---

### Sprint Retrospective

Purpose:

Improve team effectiveness.

Maximum Duration:

3 hours for a one-month Sprint.

Participants:

Scrum Team

Outputs:

* Actionable improvements
* Process adaptations

Focus:

People, practices, tools, collaboration, and quality

---

## Product Backlog Refinement

Definition:

Ongoing activity for preparing future work.

Objectives:

* Clarify requirements.
* Split large items.
* Improve estimates.
* Identify dependencies.
* Ensure readiness.

Characteristics:

* Continuous.
* Not an official Scrum event.

## Anti-Patterns

### Product Owner as Project Manager

Problem:

Focus shifts from value to task tracking.

---

### Scrum Master as Team Manager

Problem:

Reduces team self-management.

---

### Daily Scrum as Status Meeting

Problem:

Developers report to management instead of planning work.

---

### Sprint Review as Demo Only

Problem:

Stakeholder feedback and adaptation are lost.

---

### Retrospectives Without Action

A common Sprint Retrospective anti-pattern is holding retrospectives that produce no action items. The team discusses problems but commits to no concrete improvement, so nothing changes and the meeting becomes waste.

Problem:

No continuous improvement occurs. Without actionable improvement items, the retrospective is theater rather than inspection and adaptation.

Solution:

End every retrospective with one to three concrete, owned action items and review them at the next retrospective.

---

### Incomplete Increment

Problem:

Technical debt accumulates and transparency decreases.

---

### Changing Sprint Goal Mid-Sprint

Problem:

Reduces focus and predictability.

---

## Success Criteria

A healthy Scrum implementation demonstrates:

* Clear Product Goal
* Meaningful Sprint Goals
* Frequent delivery of Done increments
* Strong stakeholder collaboration
* Self-managing teams
* High transparency
* Continuous adaptation
* Continuous improvement
* Sustainable pace
* Measurable value delivery
